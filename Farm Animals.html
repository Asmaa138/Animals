Sheep

Sheep are herbivores. Lambs walk just minutes after they are born, though they are still dependent on their mothers for the first four to six months of their lives. Sheep can recognize up to 50 other sheep faces and remember them for two years.

Cow

A cow will chew about 50 times in a minute, making their jaws move about 40,000 times a day. The main stomach of a cow, the rumen, holds up to 50 gallons of food that has been partially digested.

Ducks

A duck can live between 2 and 12 years, depending on the species. Ducks have 3 eyelids. They are found on every continent except Antarctica. Since ducks have no nerves or blood vessels in their feet so they cannot feel hot or cold.

Horses

Male horses have more teeth than female horses. Horses only sleep about 3 hours a day. Horses can sleep standing up as well as laying down. A horse's average life span is 20-25 years.
